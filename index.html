Successful stories of Archbishop Peter Munde

Read The Full Story

Archbishop Peter Munde Yacoub was instrumental in fostering peace and reconciliation in South Sudan. One notable example of his efforts occurred in March 2016 at Ikpiro Parish in Yambio. During a service, Archbishop Munde facilitated the reconciliation of 40 individuals, including four pastors and eight members of the Mothers Union, who partook in Holy Communion as a symbol of their renewed unity. He emphasized that such acts of forgiveness and reconciliation are vital for achieving lasting peace in the region. ​

Additionally, Archbishop Munde's leadership contributed to creating "pockets of peace" in areas plagued by tribal conflict and war. His commitment to the Gospel's principles of forgiveness and reconciliation transformed communities, turning potential conflict zones into examples of healing and unity. ​AFRECS These instances highlight Archbishop Munde's dedication to promoting peace and unity in South Sudan through active reconciliation efforts and community engagement.

About Us

Archbishop Peter Munde Yacoub was a visionary leader in the Episcopal Church of South Sudan, known for his unwavering commitment to peace, reconciliation, and community development. Born in Yambio, South Sudan, Archbishop Munde was ordained as a deacon in 1984 and went on to serve as the Archbishop of the Internal Province of Western Equatoria

Throughout his ministry, he championed the cause of peace, organizing events like the reconciliation conferences in Yambio, where he brought together diverse community leaders to heal divisions caused by conflict. He was a vocal advocate for forgiveness, urging South Sudanese to embrace unity and nonviolence.

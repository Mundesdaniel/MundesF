Our History

Archbishop Peter Munde Yacoub was a prominent figure in the Episcopal Church of South Sudan. Born in Yambio in 1951, he pursued his secondary education at the East Africa Commercial School in Kampala, Uganda, due to the conflicts in his homeland.

He was ordained as a deacon in 1984 and became a priest the following year. In December 2016, he was installed as the Archbishop of the Internal Province of Western Equatoria and also served as the Dean of the Province of the Episcopal Church of South Sudan.

Archbishop Munde was known for his dedication to peace and reconciliation efforts in South Sudan. He actively participated in initiatives aimed at ending the ongoing conflicts and was recognized for his commitment to promoting harmony among communities. In February 2018, he supported Pope Francis's call for a day of prayer and fasting for peace in South Sudan and the Democratic Republic of Congo, emphasizing the power of faithful prayer to overcome the "evil war and the killing of innocent people." ​

Tragically, Archbishop Munde passed away on October 27, 2018, at the Coptic Hospital in Nairobi after a brief illness. His death was a significant loss to the church and the communities he served. He is remembered for his unwavering commitment to peace, his spiritual leadership, and his efforts to bring people together during challenging times.

Born on August 23, 1968, in Maridi, South Sudan, Bishop Moses Zungo has a comprehensive educational background, including a Bachelor’s Degree in Public Administration and Management from Uganda Christian University and diplomas in Biblical Studies.

Rt Rev Wilson Kamani

Diocese of Ibba

Bishop Wilson Kamani leads the Diocese of Ibba, one of the newer and less resourced dioceses in South Sudan. The diocese faces challenges such as poverty, poor infrastructure, limited education, and inadequate healthcare.

About Us

Archbishop Peter Munde Yacoub was a visionary leader in the Episcopal Church of South Sudan, known for his unwavering commitment to peace, reconciliation, and community development. Born in Yambio, South Sudan, Archbishop Munde was ordained as a deacon in 1984 and went on to serve as the Archbishop of the Internal Province of Western Equatoria

Throughout his ministry, he championed the cause of peace, organizing events like the reconciliation conferences in Yambio, where he brought together diverse community leaders to heal divisions caused by conflict. He was a vocal advocate for forgiveness, urging South Sudanese to embrace unity and nonviolence.
